Beef/Pork Giniling

Description

Beef giniling was one of the dishes that I made almost every week because of its addicting flavor and its ultra low effort to make. I could whip this dish up in 30 minutes, and it would taste so good for the amount of effort I put into it.

Ideally, this recipe uses tomato sauce, but I find that you can substitute a tomato based pasta sauce instead and it always does the trick. I personally never used tomato sauce because we never had it in our pantry lol.

Serves 4-6 people. Cooking Time: ~30 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs ground beef/pork
  • 2 medium carrots, diced
  • 3 medium potatoes, diced
  • 2 large bell peppers, diced
  • tbsp soy sauce
  • 1/8 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 cup tomato sauce/pasta sauce
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp white sugar

Instructions

  1. In a pan or skillet, sautee garlic on medium-high heat until fragrant.
  2. Add ground beef and cook until beef is browned and not too oily.
  3. Season with salt and pepper, and add carrots, potatoes, and bell peppers.
  4. Add soy sauce, and mix everything together.
  5. After around 5 minutes, add the tomato sauce, and let simmer.
  6. After around 15 minutes, add sugar and more soy sauce to taste.
  7. Let vegetables get soft, then serve with white rice!

Some notes

I would say that for this dish especially, you can very much just eyeball everything. I promise it'll turn out fine. Also, the bell peppers aren't really required, but I would recommend having at least potatoes and carrots. If you're feeling fancy, you can add peas and/or raisins.
