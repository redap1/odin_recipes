Beef Kaldereta

Description

Beef Kaldereta is a dish that is served on special occassions, and it never fails to disappoint. The tender meat with a rich stew and tangy tomato-based sauce combine to make a heavenly medley in your mouth!

This is a strong contender for my favorite dish. It does take a little more effort to make, but I promise that it's worth it. This dish is that good.

Serves 6-8 people. Cooking Time: ~3-4 hours

Ingredients

  • 2 large carrots, cut into bite-sized chunks
  • 3 potatoes, cut into bite-sized chunks
  • 1 onion, minced
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 bell peppers, cute into chunks
  • 2 1/2 lbs beef chuck, cut into bite-sized chunks
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 85 grams liver spread
  • 14 oz crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 cups beef stock
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 3 thai chillies, halved
  • 1/2 cup green olives, pitted (optional)
  • 1/3 cup frozen peas (optional)

Instructions

  1. Season cut beef chunks with salt and pepper to taste.
  2. In a pot, heat oil on high heat and sear beef chunks well.
  3. Set aside the beef, and in the same pot, sautee garlic and onion on medium-high heat.
  4. Add tomato paste and stir around for a few minutes, adding water if too dry
  5. Add liver spread and crushed tomatoes, mixing around until fully incoporated.
  6. Add back the beef and cover with beef stock.
  7. Add soy sauce, oyster sauce, fish sauce, sugar, and thai chillies.
  8. Bring to a simmer and let simmer for 2-4 hours, until the meat is super tender.
  9. While pot is simmering, pan-fry or deep-fry carrots, potatoes, and bell peppers.
  10. After simmering, add potatoes, carrots, and peppers, as well as olives and peas if you have them.
  11. Once warmed through, serve with white rice!

Some notes

There are many variations of this dish, and one of my favorite ones is adding a scoop of peanut butter and pickles. It sounds weird, but it works so well.

With the tomato paste and crushed tomatoes, you can play around with their ratios until you achieve a mixture that you enjoy. Add more tomato paste for a thicker stew, but I wouldn't recomment going less than 2 tbsp.
